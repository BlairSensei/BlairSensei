My Cocktails

Android Application


Client: Matthew Duffin

Duration: Ongoing

My Role: sketching, wireframes, user interface design, icon design, image optimization

Tools: Sketch, Photoshop, Illustrator


Google Play Link

The Challenge

Android Developer Matthew Duffin came to me for some help on a small project he was working on. He had the idea to create a cocktail recipe app, as he was dissatisfied with the current apps on available on Android. Matthew hoped to use his Android development skills and his previous cocktail bartender experience to solve this problem.


The Approach

Research and Wireframes

I began by researching the UI elements available and the current best practices on the platform. One goal for the developer was to keep the app as simple as possible and use only standard Material Design components. I began by sketching out some ideas on paper, checked in with the developer, and made iterations. I eventually made wireframes in Sketch App.



The Solution

The Look

Once the application layout was decided upon, it was time to work on polishing up the visuals. Many of the current apps now are either too busy or have no styling at all. I decided a dark grey theme with yellow accents would be a subtle and mature look. I also created the simple martini glass logo which would fit right in to the flat design look of the Android OS.

Updates

Feedback started coming in right when the app was launched. A number of the suggestions will take a while to flush out, but a couple features were put in pretty quickly. As Matthew is from England, the app started with all measurements in metric units. A toggle was added to the drink screen so users can switch between metric and imperial. The ability to add cocktails to a favorites list was also added.


Next Steps

More features have been requested, but as this is a side project for one developer, they may take some time to get to. The most requested feature is the ability for users to add and share their own cocktail recipes. MyCocktails currently only has the official cocktails of the IBA (International Bartenders Association). While this list includes a number of famous drinks, many other popular drinks are not on the list. The ability to add and share cocktail recipes would allow users to discover many new drinks and share original or modified versions of classics.
