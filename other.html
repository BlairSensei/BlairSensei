Design & Randomness

Isometric Coffee Shop

One of my favorite things to do in my free time is hang out at coffee shops. I had the idea of doing isometric illustrations of some of the best cafes I have been to. This may become a series.

This cafe is Arabica Kyoto Arashiyama, located in Kyoto, Japan. I love this place. It overlooks Mount Arashi, the Togetsukyo Bridge, and is in walking distance to a famous bamboo forest. Super cool place. Check it out if you are ever in the area.

Letterpress Designs

These are some of the designs I did while working at a letterpress stationery (NOT STATIONARY!!!) printing studio. These were made with super-fancy paper, Japanese silkscreen envelope liners and always done by hand. We sometimes used gold foil edging, embossing/debossing and other cool processes.

We used hand-fed Chandler & Price letterpresses (state of the art 19th century technology!). I did design work as well as helping out with printing. Nothing is quite as relaxing as putting your hands in a moving, metal smashing machine. It’s actually quite fun once you get the rhythm down. Die cutting can also be done with the presses. The Challenge Paper Cutter was a beast as well.

This job has made me a stationery snob.







Have a Lovely Day

"Have a Lovely Day" was a little project I did in an effort to spread a bit happiness. I created t-shirt, posters, and other items with simple, fun, and positive images in the hopes of bringing some joy to people. The posters and t-shirts were screen printed at Standard Deluxe. in Waverly, Alabama (where I was working at the time). I learned a lot about design and production during this product. Friends and family embarrass me to this day by wearing the shirts in my presence.






Quiltmaking

A number of years ago (during a short period of unemployment), I took on a small project. I decided to take a stab at what most men in their late twenties only dream of doing, making a quilt.

I went to the local quilt store in my hometown (it turns out there are actually quilt stores) and picked up some fabric. I jumped on Illustrator next and came up with a horribly-complicated design, which is probably not the best idea for my first (and likely only) quilt project. After lots of math, measuring, cutting, and learning to use a sewing machine, I completed my quilt.

I’m pretty happy with how it turned out, but it was way more expensive and time consuming to make than I expected. Never again.
