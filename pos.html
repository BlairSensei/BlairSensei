The Challenge


I was asked to help lay the groundwork for a point of sales platform. I worked with the product owner closely to figure out the needed functions of the POS suite. This new POS system would work with our existing restaurant inventory systems, allowing the managers to better see what items are being ordered, the amount of stock each store needs to keep on hand, and hopefully minimize waste of overstocked, perishable items.

Getting Started

initial point of sales sketches

I began my research with competitive and comparative analysis of existing POS platforms, as well as conducting numerous stakeholder interviews with the product owner to discuss the functionality needed by the users of our existing materials management platform. Next, I began working on some visuals to start structuring the POS system and understanding what functionality was needed.

First, I made this chart to help us iron out basic functionality for the point of sales platform. We were able to clarify our MVP (Minimum Viable Product), as well as identify less necessary features which were set as stretch goals for later updates.

mvp chart

Since the POS program needs to work with our existing inventory management software, the product owner and I worked together with backend developers to better understand the data these programs will be sharing. I created a chart to show what information fields each function POS needed to use and or display.

information chart

Can I take your order?

Once we had the groundwork completed, I started to create a flowchart to see the user flow of creating an order. While creating it, I got a better understanding of the difference between creating an eat in and take out order, as well as modifying existing orders.

wireflow diagram

Putting It All Together

Making wireframes of the order interface was my next step. Here I was able to implement all the knowledge I learned from my previous team discussions and research. It was very helpful to start seeing how the information would be inputted and displayed.

point of sales wireframes
